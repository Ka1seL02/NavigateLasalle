Our Journey

History of DLSU-D

1977

Foundation of GEAC-Cavite

On July 18, 1977, the institution was established as General Emilio Aguinaldo College (GEAC)–Cavite, under the management of Dr. Paulo C. Campos and the Yaman Lahi Foundation. It offered courses in education, business administration, nursing, midwifery, and allied health fields, marking the beginning of its journey in Cavite.

1978

Early Growth

The small library of GEAC, initially just a classroom, was moved to the administration building. This laid the foundation for a more formal information resource center, supporting the growing academic community.

1980

Expansion of Technical Courses

Around this period, technical and vocational courses were added to the curriculum, providing affordable, skills-based education to students and strengthening the college's role in practical training.

1987

Lasallian Integration

GEAC was transferred under the management of the Frère Benilde Romançon Educational Foundation, a sister corporation of De La Salle University. It became a Lasallian institution and was renamed DLSU-EAC, embracing Catholic Lasallian values.

1994

Institutional Development

The Aklatang Emilio Aguinaldo (library) was inaugurated, reflecting growth in academic infrastructure and support for a larger student population. The institution deepened its Lasallian identity and academic offerings.

2000

Founding Beginnings (Lasallian Era)

By the year 2000, DLSU-D had matured significantly under Lasallian management. This period marked a new era of growth, consolidation of programs, and preparation for greater autonomy as a leading Lasallian university in Cavite.

2010

Recognition & Excellence

DLSU-D achieved recognition as a Center of Excellence in the College of Education. The university also received Level IV accreditation and continued excelling in licensure exams and sustainability initiatives.

Today

Present-Day DLSU-D

Today, De La Salle University–Dasmariñas is a fully established Lasallian Catholic university in Cavite, emphasizing faith, zeal, and communion. It is recognized for its academic excellence, sustainability programs, research initiatives, and community engagement.
